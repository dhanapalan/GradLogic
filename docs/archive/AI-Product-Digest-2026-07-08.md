# AI & Product Digest — July 8, 2026

## 1. Anthropic overtakes OpenAI on revenue
Anthropic hit a $47B annualized revenue run rate as of May 2026, ahead of OpenAI's disclosed $25–33B. **Why it matters:** the API/enterprise market is consolidating around Anthropic faster than expected — worth factoring into any build-vs-buy or vendor-lock decisions for AI-embedded features.

## 2. Xiaomi's MiMo-V2-Pro tops OpenRouter usage
MiMo-V2-Pro is now the most-used model on OpenRouter by weekly tokens (21.1% share, 4.21T tokens/week), overtaking OpenAI's 7.5%. **Why it matters:** cheap, capable Chinese open models are winning on cost-sensitive, high-volume inference — a real alternative for PMs evaluating model routing/fallback strategy, not just a novelty.

## 3. Claude Sonnet 5 and Fable 5 pricing shifts
Sonnet 5 (launched June 30) scores 63.2% on SWE-bench Pro and beats Opus 4.8 on Terminal-Bench 2.1, priced at $2/$10 per M tokens through Aug 31. Separately, Fable 5 moved to paid usage credits ($10/$50 per M tokens) starting July 8. **Why it matters:** coding-agent quality keeps climbing while pricing gets more usage-tiered — model cost modeling is now a recurring PM/finance conversation, not a one-time decision.

## 4. GPT-5.6 in restricted preview, Gemini 3.5 Pro incoming
GPT-5.6 (Sol/Terra/Luna) launched June 26 in a government-coordinated limited preview (~20 partner orgs), with GA expected mid-July. Gemini 3.5 Pro is also expected this month. **Why it matters:** two major frontier releases landing within weeks of each other — expect another round of "which model" benchmarking pressure on any team building LLM features.

## 5. First fully autonomous AI ransomware attack documented
Sysdig published analysis of JADEPUFFER, an AI agent that chained a full ransomware lifecycle from a single unpatched exposed service with no human in the loop. **Why it matters:** agentic AI security risk just went from theoretical to documented and operational — if your product ships agents with any write/execution access, this is now a board-level risk conversation, not a hypothetical.

## 6. Cloudflare forces AI crawlers to separate search from training use
Starting Sept 15, Cloudflare will block "mixed-use" crawlers (search + AI training) from ad-supported pages unless companies split them out. **Why it matters:** if your product relies on web content ingestion or is itself indexed/scraped, this reshapes both your data-sourcing risk and your own site's discoverability by AI answer engines.

## 7. PM role is splitting, AI adoption now universal among PMs
Industry surveys show 100% of enterprise PMs use AI tools, 94% daily, saving ~4 hrs/task on PRDs, competitive analysis, and roadmaps. Coaches report the generalist PM role fracturing into more specialized tracks, with ~42K open PM roles on LinkedIn (2x last year). **Why it matters:** AI fluency has moved from differentiator to baseline expectation — the differentiation is shifting to strategic/business judgment and how fast your org updates its beliefs.

---

## Thing to watch
**AI-mediated discovery.** Products are increasingly evaluated and recommended by AI systems (search, comparisons, buying guidance) before a human ever sees them. "SEO for AI answer engines" is becoming a real discipline — worth tracking how your product's discoverability holds up when the customer's first touchpoint is an LLM, not your landing page.
